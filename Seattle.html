Where to eat

Go to brunch in trendy Capitol Hill neighborhood, a hipster paradise. Enjoy a mimosa while you people watch. I spotted a boa with a fox head, a letter jacket with a cat stitched on the back, and enough flannel to transport me back to 1994. Then, for dinner, try Ivar's Salmon house in Wallingford. Eat dinner by the window overlooking the lake and sample some of their delicious seafood dishes.

Where to stay

Seattle has many different neighborhoods to explore, each with its own ambiance and entertainment options. Visit the beautiful and classy Queen Anne's neighborhood, where you can get a gorgeous view of the city. Explore the University of Washington campus and shop in the University District, and don't miss the Troll under the bridge in Fremont.

Where to go

Must see tourist attractions include the space needle, the Chihuly Museum, and Pike Place market, where you can find the original Starbucks down the road and around the corner from the Starbucks where all the tourists are taking pictures.
